I started university two and a half years ago with an old and clunky 2013 Macbook Pro after having
used Windows for most of my school years. I still remember having to relearn most of the basic shortcuts 
for example using 'Shift' + 'Option' + '4' for screenshots instead of 'Win' + 'Shift' + 'S'
and generally getting used to the FreeBSD based macOS.

At first, I really liked the more polished, "glossy" look of macOS and used a
lot of trackpad gestures. But the window management was pure pain.

On Windows, you could arrange panes with 'Win' + 'Arrow' effortlessly, but on
macOS i found myself using the trackpad and aiming at the button in the top
right to select split view .

After spending some time on Wikipedia and fallin into a bit of a rabbit hole,
I discovered Linux with its tiling window managers like i3. The idea of
navigating everything with keyboard shortcuts and vim-like bindings
immediately clicked. If you have learned the "Language of navigation" in order to be a
bit more efficient in editing your misspelled text and correcting your code
for a text editor, you could also use this systematic approach on the
operating system level.

You don't even need a fancy mouse anymore and your handache can't escalate
into using something weird like that to click your files, because your hands
remain perfectly relaxed on your keyboard and aren't task switching all the time

picture [~/hschoss.github.io/ergonomic-mouse.jpg]

This thought process lead to me trying to install linux on a 2015 Hp Probook,
after my mac battery wasn't working and the power cable caught fire while
writing a paper in the university library.

I would say I spent about two to three weeks in general to make it work and it
is far from finished, but it never will be finished anyway.
The idea is not a final product but a infrastructure for personalization.
So I'm quite happy with my configuration at the moment..

Now i quite like my configuration, even when it is really basic. It ain't
pretty.It is simple and just works which is exactly what i need day to day.
